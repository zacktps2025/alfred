# Alfred Clients — Client Relationship Intelligence

> Treat every client like they could refer you 10 more. Because they can.

## When to Activate

Use this skill when:
- Onboarding a new client
- Writing client communications or status updates
- Assessing client health or churn risk
- Planning upsells or retention strategies
- Setting up client management processes

## Client Onboarding Framework

### The 72-Hour Window
The first 72 hours determine whether a client becomes a raving fan or a churn statistic. Front-load the experience.

### Day 0: Welcome
- Send welcome packet (scope, timeline, team contacts, communication norms)
- Create client record with all contact info, contract terms, billing context
- Set up project structure (tasks, milestones, first deliverables)
- Introduce the team members who will touch their work

### Day 1: Kickoff Call
- Confirm goals, KPIs, and success definition
- Walk through the timeline and first milestone
- Agree on communication rhythm (weekly update? Slack channel? Email only?)
- Document everything in client record — verbal agreements become written agreements

### Day 3: First Value
- Deliver something tangible. Even if small. A quick audit, a first draft, a setup confirmation.
- The psychology: reciprocity kicks in when they receive value before the first invoice hits.

### Day 7: Check-In
- Proactive call or message: "How's everything going? Any questions?"
- This catches friction before it becomes frustration.

## Communication Protocol

### The Rule of Proactivity
Never let a client wonder what's happening. If they have to ask "what's the status?" — you already lost.

### Update Frequency by Project Type
| Project Type | Update Frequency | Format |
|-------------|-----------------|--------|
| Retainer (ongoing) | Weekly | Written summary + metrics |
| Project (fixed scope) | At each milestone | Progress report |
| Campaign launch | Daily during launch week | Quick Slack/email |
| Crisis/emergency | Multiple times per day | Phone + written |

### Status Update Structure
Every client update follows this format:
1. **What shipped** — completed deliverables since last update
2. **What's in progress** — current work and expected completion
3. **What's blocked** — anything waiting on client input
4. **What's next** — upcoming work and dates
5. **Action items for client** — clear asks with deadlines

### Tone Guidelines
- Confident, not defensive
- Specific, not vague ("We completed 3 of 5 ad variations" not "Making good progress")
- Problems reported with solutions attached ("X is delayed because Y. We've already started Z to resolve it.")
- Never blame the client in writing, even if it's their fault. Reframe as "waiting on approval for X"

## Client Health Scoring

### Score Dimensions (1-5 each)

| Dimension | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Responsiveness | How quickly do they reply to requests? |
| Satisfaction | Sentiment from last communication |
| Payment | Are invoices paid on time? |
| Scope Stability | How often do they change requirements? |
| Growth Potential | Likelihood of expanding services |

### Health Thresholds
- **20-25**: Healthy. Nurture the relationship.
- **15-19**: Watch. Something needs attention.
- **10-14**: At Risk. Proactive outreach required. Investigate root cause.
- **Below 10**: Critical. Founder involvement. Save or plan graceful exit.

### Leading Indicators of Churn
- Response time increasing (they used to reply in hours, now days)
- Skipping scheduled calls
- Payment delays (first time is a red flag, second time is a pattern)
- New stakeholder introduced who wasn't part of onboarding
- "We're re-evaluating our priorities" language

## Retention Strategies

### The Quarterly Business Review
Every 90 days, schedule a 30-minute review:
- Show ROI of work delivered (use their metrics, not yours)
- Preview next quarter's plan
- Ask: "What's your biggest challenge right now that we aren't helping with?"
- This question surfaces upsell opportunities AND shows you care beyond the contract

### The Surprise Overdeliver
Once per quarter, deliver something the client didn't ask for:
- A competitive analysis they didn't request
- An optimization you noticed and fixed proactively
- A strategy recommendation beyond scope

Cost: 2 hours of work. Return: 12+ months of retention.

### The Exit Interview
When a client churns:
- Always ask why (call, not email)
- Document the reason in client record
- Look for patterns across churns — if 3 clients leave for the same reason, it's a system problem

## Contract Management

### What Every Contract Must Include
- Scope of work (specific deliverables, not vague descriptions)
- Timeline with milestones
- Payment terms (net 15 is ideal, net 30 is standard, net 60 is a bank loan)
- Change order process (how out-of-scope work gets handled)
- Termination clause (30-day notice minimum)
- IP ownership (who owns the work product)

### Change Order Protocol
When a client asks for something outside scope:
1. Acknowledge the request warmly
2. Confirm it's outside the current agreement
3. Provide a quote and timeline for the additional work
4. Get written approval before starting
5. Never do free work "to keep them happy" — it sets a permanent expectation
