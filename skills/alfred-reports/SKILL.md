# Alfred Reports — Agency Reporting Intelligence

> Reports that make decisions obvious, not just data visible.

## When to Activate

Use this skill when:
- Generating client status updates
- Creating weekly or monthly reports
- Building KPI dashboards
- Preparing financial summaries
- Running team performance reviews

## Report Types

### 1. Client Status Update (Weekly)
**Audience**: Client stakeholders
**Tone**: Confident, transparent, action-oriented
**Length**: 1 page max

```
# [Client Name] — Weekly Update
**Week of [Date]**

## Completed This Week
- [Deliverable 1] — [brief result or status]
- [Deliverable 2] — [brief result or status]

## In Progress
- [Work item] — Expected completion: [date]
- [Work item] — Expected completion: [date]

## Waiting on Client
- [Item] — Need [specific input] by [date]

## Next Week
- [Planned deliverable 1]
- [Planned deliverable 2]

## Metrics Snapshot
| Metric | This Week | Last Week | Trend |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| [KPI 1] | [value] | [value] | [up/down/flat] |
| [KPI 2] | [value] | [value] | [up/down/flat] |
```

### 2. Morning Briefing (Daily, Internal)
**Audience**: Founder / ops lead
**Tone**: Quick, scannable, actionable
**Length**: 5 bullets max

```

OPEN: [X] issues across [Y] projects
STALE: [X] items with no update in 48h+
BLOCKED: [list any blockers]
TODAY'S PRIORITIES: [top 3 items]
TEAM: [any capacity concerns]
```

## KPI Framework

### Agency-Level KPIs
| KPI | How to Calculate | Healthy Range |
|-----|-----------------|---------------|
| Revenue per employee | Total revenue / headcount | $150K-250K/year |
| Gross margin | (Revenue - direct costs) / Revenue | 50-70% |
| Client retention rate | Clients retained / total clients | >85% |
| Average project margin | Per-project margin | >40% |
| Utilization rate | Billable hours / total hours | 65-75% |
| Client acquisition cost | Sales + marketing spend / new clients | < 1 month of revenue |
| Average revenue per client | Total revenue / active clients | Growing quarter over quarter |

### Client-Level KPIs (by Service)

**Paid Media:**
- ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
- CPA (Cost per Acquisition)
- CTR (Click-Through Rate)
- Conversion Rate
- Ad Spend Efficiency

**SEO:**
- Organic traffic growth
- Keyword rankings (top 3, top 10)
- Domain authority
- Backlinks acquired
- Organic conversion rate

**Social Media:**
- Follower growth rate
- Engagement rate
- Reach / Impressions
- Content performance (top posts)
- Referral traffic from social

**Web Development:**
- Page load time
- Core Web Vitals (LCP, FID, CLS)
- Conversion rate
- Bounce rate
- Mobile vs. desktop performance

## Reporting Rules

### The 3-Second Rule
A reader should understand the headline insight in 3 seconds. If they have to read the whole report to know whether things are good or bad, the report failed.

### Lead with the Takeaway
Never bury the conclusion. Start every report with: "Here's what happened, here's what it means, here's what we should do."

### Compare to Something
Raw numbers are meaningless. Always show:
- This period vs. last period (trend)
- Actual vs. target (performance)
- You vs. industry benchmark (context)

### Visual Hierarchy
- Green = good / on track
- Yellow = watch / slightly off
- Red = action needed / below target
- Use color consistently across all reports

### Never Include
- Vanity metrics without context (impressions alone mean nothing)
- Data without interpretation ("Clicks increased 15%" — so what?)
- Excuses without solutions ("Traffic dropped because..." — and here's what we're doing about it)
