# Alfred Sales — Outreach & Closing Intelligence

> Outreach that reads like a message from a peer, not a pitch from a stranger.

## When to Activate

Use this skill when:
- Prospecting for new clients
- Writing cold outreach (email, DM, LinkedIn)
- Building follow-up sequences
- Drafting proposals or SOWs
- Handling objections
- Writing sales scripts for calls

## Prospecting Framework

### The ICP Filter (Ideal Client Profile)
Before reaching out to anyone, run them through this filter:

| Criteria | Question | Disqualify If |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| Budget | Can they afford your minimum retainer? | Revenue below $500K/year |
| Need | Do they have a pain you solve? | No visible marketing activity |
| Authority | Are you talking to the decision maker? | "I'll need to check with my boss" |
| Timeline | Do they need results now? | "Maybe next quarter" |
| Fit | Would you enjoy working with them? | Red flags in communication |

### Where to Find Prospects
1. **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** — filter by industry, company size, role
2. **Competitor followers** — people engaging with your competitors' content
3. **Job boards** — companies hiring for the role you replace (hiring "marketing manager" = need agency)
4. **Industry communities** — Slack groups, Facebook groups, Reddit
5. **Referrals** — ask every happy client: "Who else do you know?"

## Cold Outreach Framework

### The SPIN Approach
Based on SPIN Selling methodology, adapted for agency outreach:

1. **Situation**: Reference something specific about their business (shows research)
2. **Problem**: Name a challenge you've observed (not assumed)
3. **Implication**: What happens if they don't solve it
4. **Need-Payoff**: What the outcome looks like with your help

### Cold Email Template
```
Subject: [Specific observation about their business]

Hey [Name],

[1 sentence about what they do that shows you researched them]

I noticed [specific problem or opportunity — be concrete, not vague].

We recently helped [similar company/industry] solve this exact problem —
[specific result with numbers].

Would it make sense to chat for 15 minutes about whether we could
do something similar for [Company]?

[Your name]
[One-line credibility: "Founder of X, we work with Y companies"]
```

### Outreach Rules
- Personalize the first line. Mass emails die in spam.
- Reference something specific: a recent blog post, a campaign they ran, a job posting
- Keep it under 100 words. Respect their time.
- One CTA. "Would a 15-minute call make sense?" That's it.
- Never attach files in cold emails. Triggers spam filters.
- Send Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM their timezone
- Subject line: lowercase, specific, 5-8 words

### Follow-Up Sequence
| Touchpoint | Timing | Channel | Message |
|-----------|--------|---------|---------|
| Initial | Day 0 | Email | The SPIN outreach |
| Follow-up 1 | Day 3 | Email | "Bumping this — [add new value/insight]" |
| Follow-up 2 | Day 7 | LinkedIn | Connect + note referencing the email |
| Follow-up 3 | Day 14 | Email | Share relevant content (case study, article) |
| Follow-up 4 | Day 21 | Email | "Last note — [restate value prop briefly]" |
| Breakup | Day 30 | Email | "Closing the loop — reach out if timing changes" |

Never follow up more than 5 times without a response. After that, move to nurture (content, not outreach).

## Proposal Writing

### The Proposal Structure
```
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (1 paragraph)
   - Who you are, what you'll do, what they'll get

2. UNDERSTANDING (1 page)
   - Restate THEIR problem in THEIR words
   - This proves you listened on the discovery call

3. APPROACH (1-2 pages)
   - What you'll do, phase by phase
   - Timeline with milestones
   - Deliverables for each phase

4. TEAM (half page)
   - Who will work on their account
   - Relevant experience of each person

5. INVESTMENT (1 page)
   - Pricing with clear scope
   - What's included and what's not
   - Payment terms

6. NEXT STEPS (3 bullets)
   - Exactly what happens after they say yes
```

### Proposal Rules
- Send within 24 hours of the discovery call. Speed wins deals.
- Lead with their problem, not your capabilities
- Include social proof: 2-3 relevant case studies or testimonials
- Price anchoring: if your rate is $5K/mo, show what hiring in-house costs ($8K+)
- Always include "What's NOT included" to prevent scope creep expectations
- Proposal should be readable in 5 minutes. Decision makers skim.

## Objection Handling

### Common Objections & Responses

**"You're too expensive."**
> "I understand budget is important. Let me ask — what would the cost of NOT solving this problem be over the next 6 months? We've found that [similar client] was losing $X/month in [specific area] before we started. The investment pays for itself in [timeframe]."

**"We've been burned by agencies before."**
> "That's a valid concern, and honestly common. Can I ask what went wrong? [Listen.] Here's how we do things differently: [specific process that addresses their bad experience]. And we have a [30-day exit clause / money-back guarantee / milestone-based payment] so you're never stuck."

**"We're going to handle it in-house."**
> "That makes sense for some businesses. Just a thought — the cost of a full-time [role] is $[X]/year plus benefits, management time, and ramp-up. We can deliver the same output for $[Y]/month with zero ramp-up time. Worth comparing?"

**"Let me think about it."**
> "Of course. What specific concerns would you want to resolve before moving forward? I want to make sure I address everything." [This surfaces the real objection.]

**"Can you send more info?"**
> "Absolutely. What specific questions would you want answered? That way I can send you exactly what's relevant." [This qualifies whether they're serious or just being polite.]

## Discovery Call Script

### The 30-Minute Framework
```
Minutes 0-5: Rapport
- "Tell me about [Company]. What are you working on right now?"

Minutes 5-15: Discovery (SPIN)
- "What's your biggest challenge with [area]?"
- "How long has this been a problem?"
- "What have you tried?"
- "What happens if nothing changes?"
- "What would success look like?"

Minutes 15-20: Pitch
- "Based on what you've shared, here's what we'd do..."
- [Tailored to their specific problems, not a generic pitch]

Minutes 20-25: Social Proof
- "We had a similar client, [Company], who [had same problem]. We [did X] and they [got Y result]."

Minutes 25-30: Close
- "Does this approach make sense for where you are?"
- If yes: "Great. I'll send a proposal by [tomorrow]. Once you review it, we can schedule a follow-up to finalize."
- If maybe: "What would need to be true for this to be a yes?"
```
