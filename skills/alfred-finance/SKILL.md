# Alfred Finance — Agency Financial Intelligence

> Revenue is vanity. Margin is sanity. Cash flow is reality.

## When to Activate

Use this skill when:
- Setting or adjusting pricing
- Analyzing project or client profitability
- Managing invoices and accounts receivable
- Planning budgets or forecasting
- Making financial decisions about the business

## Pricing Strategy

### The Three Pricing Models

**1. Hourly Billing**
- When: early-stage agency, undefined scope, ad-hoc work
- Pro: simple, low risk
- Con: punishes efficiency (faster = less revenue), income cap
- Rate formula: (desired salary / 1,000 billable hours) x 2.5 = hourly rate
- Example: $150K target / 1,000 hours x 2.5 = $375/hour

**2. Project-Based (Fixed Fee)**
- When: well-defined scope, repeatable deliverables
- Pro: predictable revenue, rewards efficiency
- Con: scope creep risk, requires accurate estimation
- Pricing formula: estimated hours x internal rate x 1.5 margin buffer
- Always include a change order clause

**3. Monthly Retainer**
- When: ongoing services, relationship-based work
- Pro: predictable recurring revenue, deeper client relationships
- Con: can become "all you can eat" without boundaries
- Structure: define included deliverables, hours, and scope clearly
- Annual lock-in with quarterly reviews is ideal

### Pricing Rules
- Never compete on price. Compete on expertise, speed, and results.
- If no one pushes back on your price, you're too cheap.
- Price the outcome, not the hours. "$50K website" not "200 hours at $250"
- Annual price increases are expected. 5-10% per year minimum.
- Offer 3 tiers: most clients pick the middle one (anchoring effect)

## Margin Analysis

### Project Profitability Formula
```
Gross Margin = (Revenue - Direct Costs) / Revenue x 100

Direct Costs include:
- Team labor (hours x internal cost rate)
- Contractor fees
- Software/tools specific to project
- Ad spend (if not passed through)
- Any direct expenses
```

### Healthy Margin Targets
| Metric | Target | Warning | Critical |
|--------|--------|---------|----------|
| Gross margin (per project) | >50% | 30-50% | <30% |
| Gross margin (agency-wide) | >55% | 40-55% | <40% |
| Net margin | >20% | 10-20% | <10% |
| Utilization rate | 65-75% | 50-65% | <50% |

### Margin Killers (Watch For These)
1. **Scope creep without change orders**: Free work erodes margin silently
2. **Underestimated projects**: Track estimated vs. actual hours on every project
3. **Context switching**: A developer on 4 projects is 30% less efficient than on 2
4. **Rework cycles**: More than 2 revision rounds = estimation or communication failure
5. **Senior resources on junior work**: Track who's doing what — misallocation kills margins

## Invoice Management

### Invoicing Rules
- Invoice on time, every time. Late invoices = late payments.
- Net 15 is ideal. Net 30 is standard. Never net 60+.
- Send invoices at the beginning of the month, not the end.
- Include a late payment fee (1.5% per month is standard).
- Retainers: invoice in advance. Projects: invoice at milestones or 50/50.

### Payment Collection Protocol
| Status | Age | Action |
|--------|-----|--------|
| Outstanding | 1 day past due | Friendly reminder email |
| Late | 7 days past due | Direct email to primary contact |
| Overdue | 15 days past due | Phone call to decision maker |
| Delinquent | 30 days past due | Pause work, formal notice |
| Collections | 60+ days past due | Final notice, consider collections |

### Cash Flow Protection
- Maintain 3 months of operating expenses as reserve
- Never depend on one client for more than 30% of revenue
- Collect deposits on new projects (50% upfront is standard for projects)
- Separate business and personal finances completely
- Track cash flow weekly, not monthly

## P&L Structure for Agencies

```
REVENUE
  Retainer revenue
  Project revenue
  Hourly/consulting revenue
  Pass-through revenue (ad spend managed)
  ---
  Total Revenue

COST OF SERVICES (Direct Costs)
  Team salaries (delivery team)
  Contractor payments
  Project-specific software
  ---
  Gross Profit

OPERATING EXPENSES
  Sales & marketing
  General & administrative
  Tools & software (general)
  Office / co-working
  Professional services (legal, accounting)
  ---
  Operating Profit (EBITDA)

OTHER
  Interest / loans
  Taxes
  ---
  Net Profit
```

## Financial Decision Framework

### When to Hire
- Current team utilization consistently above 80% for 2+ months
- You're turning away work you want to take
- Revenue can support the hire for 6 months even if no new clients come in
- The hire will increase capacity by more than their cost

### When to Raise Prices
- Your margins are below 50%
- You have a waitlist or are turning away clients
- Your rates haven't increased in 12+ months
- You've significantly improved your skills or results
- Existing clients are getting more value than they're paying for

### When to Fire a Client
- They consistently pay late (3+ times)
- Margin on their work is below 20%
- They consume disproportionate management time
- The team dreads working on their account
- They're abusive or disrespectful to your team
