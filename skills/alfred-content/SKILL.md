# Alfred Content — Content Production Engine

> Every piece of content solves one problem for one person. That's the whole game.

## When to Activate

Use this skill when:
- Writing blog posts, LinkedIn posts, or social media content
- Creating email campaigns or newsletters
- Writing video scripts (VSL, explainers, social)
- Repurposing existing content across platforms
- Building a content calendar

## Core Philosophy

### The Three Rules
1. **Problem-first**: Every piece starts with a specific pain point. Not your solution. Their problem.
2. **One piece, one idea**: If you have three insights, write three pieces. Never dilute.
3. **Founder voice, not corporate voice**: Direct, specific, credible. Write like a person, not a brand.

### Forbidden Words
These words trigger AI detection and sound generic:
- streamline, empower, game-changer, robust, leverage (as verb), synergy
- revolutionary, cutting-edge, next-gen, best-in-class, world-class
- unlock potential, drive results, take it to the next level
- seamless, holistic, disruptive, innovative (without specifics)

Replace with: specific numbers, concrete examples, honest language.

## LinkedIn Posts (150-300 words)

### Structure
```
HOOK — specific pain point or counterintuitive observation (NOT a question)

BODY — 100-200 words in short paragraphs (1-3 sentences each)
Each paragraph advances one point.

CLOSE — directional statement, not hard CTA
```

### Rules
- Never start with "I" — platforms de-prioritize this
- One insight per post
- No hashtag spam — 3 max, placed at the very end
- Use line breaks generously — walls of text get scrolled past
- Personal stories outperform advice 3:1

### Hook Formulas
- "[Bold statement that challenges conventional wisdom]"
- "Last week I [did something] and here's what I learned..."
- "Most [role] get [thing] wrong. Here's what actually works."
- "[Surprising number] of [audience] don't know that [insight]."

## Blog Posts (600-1200 words)

### Architecture
```
Problem (1 section) — Hook with the pain point
Context (1 section) — Why this problem exists
Solution (1-2 sections) — Your framework or approach
Implication (1 section) — What changes when they apply this
```

### Rules
- Open with the problem as hook — first paragraph must earn attention
- Each H2 section: 100-200 words, one idea, ends with a point
- No H1 in body (that's the page title) — start with H2
- Weave target keyword naturally — no keyword stuffing
- Internal links to 2-3 related pieces
- End with a single, clear next step
- Never pitch directly in blog content — frame product as context for solution

### SEO Essentials
- Title: under 60 characters, keyword front-loaded
- Meta description: 150-160 chars, includes keyword and CTA
- URL slug: 3-5 words, hyphenated, keyword included
- At least one image with alt text
- First keyword appearance in first 100 words

## Email Campaigns

### Subject Line Rules
- Under 50 characters (40% of opens happen on mobile)
- Specific, never clickbait
- Lowercase feels personal ("quick question about your agency")
- Numbers and specifics outperform vague ("3 things we fixed this week")

### Email Body
- 100-200 words max for marketing emails
- One clear CTA. Not two. One.
- Write like you're emailing one person, not a list
- P.S. lines get read more than body copy — use them for the CTA

### Email Types for Agencies
| Type | Purpose | Frequency |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Value email | Teach something useful | Weekly |
| Case study | Show results | Bi-weekly |
| Offer email | Pitch your service | Monthly max |
| Personal story | Build connection | When you have one |
| Curation | Share useful resources | Bi-weekly |

## Video Scripts

### VSL Structure (Video Sales Letter)
```
Hook (0-15 sec) — Name the problem. Grab attention.
Problem (15-45 sec) — Agitate the pain. Show you understand.
Solution (45-90 sec) — Introduce your approach. Show the transformation.
Proof (90-120 sec) — Testimonials, results, credibility.
Offer (120-150 sec) — What they get. Stack the value.
CTA (150-180 sec) — One clear action. Tell them exactly what to do.
```

### Short-Form Video (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)
- 30-60 seconds max
- Hook in first 2 seconds or lose them
- One tip, one insight, one story per video
- End with an open loop or CTA ("Comment X if you want the template")
- Captions required — 85% watch without sound

## Content Repurposing Engine

### The 1-to-7 Method
One long-form piece becomes 7+ distribution pieces:

1. **Blog post** (1200 words) — the anchor piece
2. **LinkedIn post** — key insight from the blog
3. **Twitter/X thread** — break the blog into 5-8 tweets
4. **Email** — summary + link to full post
5. **Short video** — 60-sec version of the key point
6. **Carousel** — visual breakdown for Instagram/LinkedIn
7. **Quote graphic** — strongest one-liner as an image

### Repurposing Rules
- Adapt the format, not just copy-paste
- Each platform has different norms — respect them
- The core insight stays the same, the packaging changes
- Track which platform-version performs best — double down there

## Content Calendar

### Weekly Cadence (Minimal)
| Day | Content | Platform |
|-----|---------|----------|
| Monday | LinkedIn post | LinkedIn |
| Tuesday | Email to list | Email |
| Wednesday | Short video | Instagram/TikTok |
| Thursday | Blog post published | Website |
| Friday | Repurpose Thursday's blog | Twitter, LinkedIn |

### Batch Production
- Write 4 LinkedIn posts in one sitting (1 hour)
- Record 4 short videos in one session (2 hours)
- Write 4 emails in one batch (1 hour)
- Batch beats daily creation every time. Context switching kills quality.
